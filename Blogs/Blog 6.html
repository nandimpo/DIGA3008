24 March 2025

Topic:Design Sections.

Style Guides:

Goal: My goal is to have a user friendly, accessible and clear website that showcases who I am as a designer.

Visual design elements:

  • I want my website to have a strong typographic hierarchy. I will use headings to organize the information and draw the user's eye to what is important. Headings will also make the information more readable. I chose the font 'Grown' for my headings because it is readable and has curved elements that resonate with the feminine feel of my website. Additionally, I selected 'Montserrat Classic' for body text because of its high readability and modern appearance.

    • Fonts - Readability and Legibility

  • I want my website to have a limited color palette with strong accent colors. This approach will make the website feel polished and clear. I chose colors that represent who I am. I selected brown because, as a Black African woman, I wanted to embrace a color that is sometimes unfairly considered unattractive. To create warmth, I chose yellow and orange to complement the brown. For accent colors, I selected cooler tones to highlight important elements and create visual contrast.

  • Visual Design Elements: I have implemented a hierarchy of information. The first thing the user will see on each wireframe is the name of the screen. Also, the information is centre-aligned to draw the viewer to the center of the page where the most important information is located. The buttons on the page are bright yellow to draw the user's focus to them.
  • Interaction Points: I have implemented buttons on almost all the pages to guide the user. On the blogs page, there are cards that highlight when the user hovers over them. Also, I have implemented a scroll bar so the user can scroll through the blog posts.
