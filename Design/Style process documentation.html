Style Process Documentation

My stylistic design decisions—from fonts to layout—documented through visual and strategic exploration.

Goal Alignment

The goal of my website is to express who I am as a designer. I want it to be clear, accessible, and warm.

Site Content Mapping

User Flow – Screens, Behaviour, and Decisions

Possible Style Designs

This is my initial design direction. While I like the warmth, I plan to further enhance it with the full palette.

Fonts – Readability and Legibility

‘Grown’ is used for headings to add personality, and ‘Montserrat Classic’ for body text due to its readability.

Colours – Theory and Application

Primary Colour Palette

I chose warm, analogous colors (yellow, orange) for harmony and unity.

Secondary Colour Palette

Secondary colors (blue/orange) add contrast and visual balance as complementary tones.

Composition – Hierarchy, Balance, Harmony

The site is symmetrically laid out and center-aligned. Circular motifs and rotational repetition support visual harmony.

Shape, Colour, and Position

Rounded and circular buttons align with my goal of creating a soft, inviting design.

Reference Material

  • Website: Dribbble – Hero Slider Example
    I admire the use of circular shapes to create a soft, welcoming atmosphere.
  • Website: Pixcap – Warm Colour Theory
    This helped me choose my palette. Warm colors evoke excitement and are effective in user engagement and brand identity.
  • Emotional impact: Red, orange, and yellow build excitement and trust.
  • Attention-grabbing: They highlight CTAs and key elements.
  • Warmth: These tones encourage longer visits and user comfort.
  • Identity: They convey friendliness and energy that matches my brand goals.
