Fonts

I choose the ‘Grown’ font for the header because it is interesting yet legible.I chose ‘Montserrat Classic’ as the body text because it is more legible and readable.

Colours - Colour theory and relationships

Colour Palette

Primary colour palette:

I chose warm colours because they are inviting. Also yellow and orange are analogous colours. Also these colours create a sense of harmony and unity that's pleasing to the eye.

Secondary colour palette:

I chose colours in the secondary colour palette to contrast the primary colour palette to create a sense of balance. Orange and blue are complementary colours as well. If these colours are used together they enhance visual appeal and make elements stand out.

Composition - Hierarchy, Balance, Harmony

My composition is symmetrical and centre aligned to create a sense of balance and harmony. Additionally I have repeated the circular elements and I have used rotation and reflection especially on the blog and portfolio screen.

Interface elements - visual representations (Shape, colour, position)

I want circular and rounded shapes so they align with my design goal of creating a welcoming and warm website.

Reference Material

I like the use of circular shapes and design that create a welcoming and soothing environment.

Inspiration Inspiration

This website helped me to choose a warm colour palette because as the website says:

  • Emotional Impact: Red, orange, and yellow evoke happiness and excitement, strengthening user-content connections and building brand loyalty.
  • Attention-Grabbing: These vibrant hues highlight important elements like CTAs, improving conversions and user engagement.
  • Welcoming Environment: Warm tones create inviting atmospheres that encourage longer visits and build trust.
  • Brand Identity: These colors convey energy and friendliness, establishing cohesive visual identities that resonate with target audiences.
